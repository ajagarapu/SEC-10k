<DIV align="center" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 18pt"><B>DuPont Enters 2009 with Strong Cash Position; Drives Productivity and Cash-Generating Actions</B><BR>
<B><I>Company&#146;s Fourth Quarter 2008 Results in Line with Guidance</I></B>
</DIV>

</DIV>


<DIV style="margin-top: 6pt">
<TABLE width="100%" border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" style="font-size: 10pt">

    <TD width="3%" nowrap align="left"><B>&#149;</B></TD>
    <TD width="1%">&nbsp;</TD>

<TR>
    <TD style="font-size: 6pt">&nbsp;</TD>

    <TD style="font-size: 6pt">&nbsp;</TD>

strong.</B></TD>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;DuPont enters 2009 addressing challenging economic conditions head-on,&#148; said DuPont CEO Ellen
J. Kullman. &#147;We are intensely focused on productivity, while generating earnings and cash. Our
market-leading businesses and internal discipline generated solid cash performance in 2008. We do
not underestimate the difficulties presented by the current environment. We will rigorously guard

advantage to assure that the company is well-positioned for an eventual improvement in global
markets.&#148;

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><U><B>Global Consolidated Sales and Net Income</B></U>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Consolidated net sales in the fourth quarter of $5.8&nbsp;billion were 17&nbsp;percent lower than prior
year, reflecting 20&nbsp;percent lower volume, 7&nbsp;percent higher local prices, 3&nbsp;percent negative impact
from currency and a 1&nbsp;percent net reduction from portfolio changes. Weaker demand across most
markets led to significantly lower global sales volume. Local pricing gains in all regions and in
all segments were more than offset by declines in volume and unfavorable currency. Sales were down
in all regions, including a 16&nbsp;percent decline in emerging markets. The table below shows

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><U><B>Outlook</B></U>
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The company expects that global macroeconomic conditions for first quarter 2009 will be
similar to fourth quarter 2008, with very weak demand in most of the company&#146;s key markets,
excluding agriculture. Earnings growth for the Agriculture &#038; Nutrition segment is expected to be
more than offset by lower earnings in the other segments. DuPont expects first quarter 2009
earnings to be in the range of $.50 to $.70 per share.
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For 2009, the company&#146;s earnings outlook is a range of $2.00 to $2.50 per share, anticipating
that the current global recession will continue in 2009. While favorable conditions in global
agriculture markets are expected in 2009, lower demand for non-agriculture products and the impact
of currency is expected to limit the company&#146;s revenue growth. The company expects to continue an
appropriate level of spending for high-growth, high-margin businesses, including seed products and
photovoltaics.
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;We are acutely focused on executing with a sense of urgency across the company,&#148; Kullman
said. &#147;To enhance our strong financial position, we implemented additional cash-generating actions
during the fourth quarter, including reduced spending and restructuring to better align capital
expenditures and costs with lower global demand. For 2009, we will deliver about $730&nbsp;million in
fixed cost reductions and about $1&nbsp;billion in reduced working capital, and we will capitalize on
opportunities that emerge in the current environment.&#148;
</DIV>
